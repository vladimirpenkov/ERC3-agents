# Markets and Customers

Bellini Coatings serves industrial customers across **Western and Central Europe**, with selected exports to the Balkans and neighbouring regions.

## Primary market segments

1. **Industrial machinery and equipment**
   - Coatings for presses, conveyors, packaging machinery, pumps and related equipment.
   - Focus on mechanical durability, lubricants and chemical resistance.

2. **Automotive and commercial vehicles**
   - Components such as brackets, frames, chassis parts and accessories.
   - Requirements for corrosion protection, stone‑chip resistance and visual quality.

3. **Rail and public transport**
   - Coatings for railcars, bogies, structural components and infrastructure (e.g. signal masts).
   - High standards for weathering, mechanical impact and maintenance intervals.

4. **Food & beverage production**
   - Coatings for tanks, piping, floors and walls in processing areas.
   - Hygienic, washable, often food‑contact compliant or low‑VOC.

5. **Industrial flooring and structural steel**
   - Floor systems for warehouses, production halls and loading areas.
   - Anti‑corrosion systems for beams, columns and external structures.

## Typical customers

- Medium to large **OEMs** (Original Equipment Manufacturers).
- Engineering companies responsible for plant construction and refurbishment.
- Contractors specialised in industrial flooring and protective coatings.
- Rail operators and their maintenance providers.

Our customers generally have:

- **Technical buying criteria** (performance, durability, compliance).
- **Long decision cycles** (testing, validation, approvals).
- A desire for **stable supply and consistent quality** over many years.

## Customer lifecycle in our systems

1. **Lead / idea**
   - Customer contact identified (trade fair, referral, inbound request).
   - Added to the CRM and represented as a `Company` record.
   - Deal phase in systems: `idea`.

2. **Exploring**
   - First visits, basic technical discussions.
   - Early lab suggestions or product recommendations.
   - Deal phase: `exploring`.

3. **Active**
   - Formal projects opened in the **project registry** for trials, custom formulations or supply schemes.
   - Significant internal time logged to the customer and related projects.
   - Deal phase: `active`.

4. **Paused or archived**
   - Projects may be `paused` (no current activity) or `archived` (closed).
   - Customer remains in CRM with a high‑level status (e.g. “Curious but cautious”, “Key account”, “Dormant”).

Our systems use these phases mainly for **visibility and prioritisation**. The exact phase is maintained by the **account manager**, typically after pipeline review meetings.
